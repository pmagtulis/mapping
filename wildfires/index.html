Entire homes in over half of counties in Texas are prone to wildfires

June 27, 2022

Wildfires are not new in the US, but some states, like Texas, experience it more than others.
Official data show that across the large swath of land in the southern state, 100% of homes settled in 143 Texan counties are either in direct or indirect risk of wildfires. That account for about 56% of the 253 counties in the state, and over 1.2 million home settlements under threat of getting destroyed.
Others also have exponentially high risk from wildfires. States At Risk, a climate project, estimated that about 18 million people in Texas suffer from wildfires annually, equivalent to 72% of the state's population.
Despite the high risk from wildfires state wide, some pockets of southern Texas have slightly lower risk from wildfires as measured by homes at risk from the phenomenon. For instance, only a little above one in four of the 142,000 housing units in Nueces county were found in danger from wildfires, data showed.
Still, State at Risk said Texas is "projected to top the nation with the worst over wildfire threat" by 2050.

Last February, the UN said wildfires are only bound to get stronger and more prevalent by 2050 due to climate change. In the US alone, over 100,000 wildfire events were recorded in the past two years, despite a broader decline in greenhouse gas emissions that cause them in 2020 when the pandemic shut down economic activities.
