How much of the world's electricity is still coming from oil?

June 19, 2022

Who's obssessed with oil?
As soaring oil prices hurt economies, there's one sector which has successfully wean itself of reliance on fossil fuels-- electricity.
For sure, higher gas prices had been hurting car owners, public transport and those industries that rely on oil as raw material such as logistics, but data showed lights at our homes are pretty insulated from the impact.
Figures from the International Energy Agency, collated by the World Bank, showed average global power production sourced from oil sources has dropped from over a fifth in 1978 to just 3.3% in 2015, the latest period for which data is available.

Out of 151 countries where data is available, only oil-producing nations in the Middle East like Saudi Arabia were found still heavily reliant on oil for their electricity needs. Low-income African countries such as Sudan were also significantly oil-dependent, data showed.
In contrast, only 0.9% of US's electricity needs were coming from oil as of 2015, Germany sourced 1%, while France got a smaller share of 0.4% of total power production from fuel. The three countries, together with the the rest of European Union, have recently banned or drastically reduced their oil imports from Russia as part of sanctions against the country for invading Ukraine.

Global oil prices have soared in recent months as a surge in demand from countries coming out of Covid-19 lockdowns was met by supply constraints due to the Ukraine crisis. Russia is one of the world's top oil exporters.
Oil is contributing to a broader surge in global inflation. On top of that, China's zero-Covid policy, which prompted it to continue with harsh lockdowns that hurt its manufacturing, is adding to the pain. Prinz Magtulis
